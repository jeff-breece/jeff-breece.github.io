# Beets on the rocks!

__Posted on[ November 4, 2014](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750/https://jeffbreece.com/beets-straight-up/ "Beets on the rocks!") by [![](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750im_/https://secure.gravatar.com/avatar/e0ec49ea47c3c9e70f0f0098fd1a4d12?s=32&d=mm&r=g)Jeff Breece](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750/https://jeffbreece.com/author/jeff/ "View all posts by Jeff Breece") Posted in [food](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750/https://jeffbreece.com/category/food/) — [No Comments ↓](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750/https://jeffbreece.com/beets-straight-up/#respond)

This was one of those fun things as a result of a gift of a Ninja mixer from Eddie’s mom. I love beets so this was a good way to enjoy the earthy flavor with a little zing of lemon as a fresh pre workout drink.

[![Beet-Juice-Strained](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750im_/https://jeffbreece.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/BeetJuiceStrain-168x300.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20190828150750/https://jeffbreece.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/BeetJuiceStrain.jpg)

Ingredients

  * 3 or 4 beets
  * 1 whole lemon
  * Filtered water



Process

  1. Process the beets and lemon in the blender until it’s a pulp mix.
  2. Using a fine strainer, strain the liquid from the pulp.
  3. Serve chilled.
